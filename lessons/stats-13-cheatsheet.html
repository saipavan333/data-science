Statistics & Probability Cheatsheet

Every statistical idea in the track on one printable page.

6 min readBeginner-friendlyLesson 12 of 12 in track

Every statistical idea in the track, distilled to one scannable page — the term, and what it actually means. Statistics is a small number of ideas used over and over; this is all of them. Press Print for a copy by your desk.

▤ CHEATSHEET

Statistics & Probability — one-page reference

Read each row as "term — the one-sentence meaning". If you can explain every row out loud, you can pass the statistics round.

Center & spread

meanaverage; pulled by outliers
medianmiddle value; resists outliers
variance, stdtypical distance from the mean
IQR = Q3 - Q1middle 50%; robust spread
skewwhich tail is longer

Probability

P(A)chance of an event, 0 to 1
P(A and B)both happen
P(A|B)A given B already happened
independentP(A|B) = P(A)
Bayesflip a conditional using the base rate

Distributions

Normalbell; 68-95-99.7 within 1-2-3 SD
Binomialcount of successes in n trials
Poissoncount of events in an interval
PMF vs PDFbars for counts, curve for measures

Sampling & the CLT

population vs sampleeveryone vs the few you measure
parameter vs statistictrue value vs your estimate
standard error = σ/√nhow much the mean wobbles
CLTsample means → normal, whatever the population
4× data → half the errorSE shrinks like 1/√n

Estimation

point estimatesingle best guess (e.g. x̄)
95% CI = est ± 1.96·SEan honest range
correct reading95% of such intervals cover the truth
bootstrapresample to get a CI without a formula

Hypothesis testing

H0 / H1no effect (skeptic) vs an effect
p-valueP(data this extreme | H0 true)
α (e.g. 0.05)false-alarm rate you accept
Type I / IIfalse positive / missed effect
power = 1 - βchance of catching a real effect
'fail to reject'not proof H0 is true

Choosing a test

t-testcompare 1 or 2 means
paired t-testbefore/after on the same units
chi-squareassociation between categories
Mann-Whitney / Wilcoxonnonparametric alternatives

Correlation & traps

Pearson rlinear strength, -1 to +1
Spearman ρmonotonic (rank) strength
r near 0no linear trend (could still curve)
always plot firstAnscombe: same r, different data
correlation ≠ causationconfounders lurk
